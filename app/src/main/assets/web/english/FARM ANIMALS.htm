Farm animals

 

ROOSTER

an adult male domestic chicken

 

 

HEN

an female chicken. Hens are usually brown or white. Both males and females have one or two wattles .

 

 

CHICK

a domestic chicken; especially : one newly hatched

 

 

PIG

Pigs are stout, barrel-shaped mammals that are known for their big appetites. Some kinds of pigs are wild, while others are domestic (raised by humans).

 

 

SHEEP

Sheep are among the most valuable of all domestic animals. Domestic animals are ones that have been tamed for use by humans. People eat sheep meat and drink

sheep milk. The wool is used to make cloth. Male sheep are called rams. Female sheep are called ewes. Sheep are closely related to goats.

 

 

LAMB

a young sheep especially less than one year old or without permanent teeth

 

 

HORSE

The horse is a mammal that people have valued for thousands of years.

In the past people commonly used horses to get from place to place and to pull heavy loads.

 

 

DONKEY

The donkey is a member of the horse family. The words donkey and ass are used to identify

the same animal. However, the term donkey is used for domesticated, or tamed, animals. The term ass is more

often used when the animal is wild.

 

 

DUCK

The waterbirds called ducks are related to geese and swans. There are about 100 species, or types,

of duck. They are found almost all over the world. Many types migrate, or fly long distances to spend

different seasons in different regions. People keep ducks for their meat, eggs, and feathers.

 

 

GOAT

Goats are horned mammals that can be either wild or tame.

People keep goats for their meat, milk, and wool. They are hardy animals that can live

on coarse, thin grass. Goats can be raised on land that does not have enough plants for cows or sheep.

 

 

CATTLE

Cattle are large, hoofed mammals that people raise for their meat, milk, or hides.

In some places cattle also pull carts or farm equipment. Cattle belong to the scientific

family Bovidae, which also includes buffalo, bison, and yaks.

 

 

CALF

the young of the domestic cattle; also : that of a closely related mammal (such as a bison)
